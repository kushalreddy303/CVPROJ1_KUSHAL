Annotated Bibliography

Key research papers and resources cited throughout this tutorial.

[1]

Sudhir Gaikwad, Shripad Bhatlawande, Swati Shilaskar, and Anjali Solanke. 2023. A computer vision-approach for activity recognition and residential monitoring of elderly people. Medicine in Novel Technology and Devices, 20 (2023), 100272. Elsevier. https://doi.org/10.1016/j.medntd.2023.100272

Synopsis: Proposes a vision-based elderly monitoring system using BlazePose landmarks to detect sitting, standing, walking, lying, and falling. Evaluates multiple ML models (SVM, RF, DT, Ensemble) with high accuracy (~99%).

Reliability: Published in an Elsevier journal, peer-reviewed, strong experimental validation with real participants.

[2]

F. Xavier Gaya-Morey, Cristina Manresa-Yee, and Jose M. Buades-Rubio. 2023. Explainable activity recognition for the elderly. In Proceedings of the XXIII International Conference on Human Computer Interaction (Interacción '23). ACM, New York, NY, USA, 8 pages. https://doi.org/10.1145/3612783.3612790

Synopsis: Uses Explainable AI (LIME) to make deep learning–based activity recognition transparent and trustworthy for caregivers. Focuses on elderly activity detection using the ETRI-Activity3D dataset.

Reliability: Peer-reviewed ACM conference paper. Trusted for methodology and novelty in combining HAR with XAI.

[3]

Abhijeet Ravankar, Arpit Rawankar, and Ankit A. Ravankar. 2023. Real-time monitoring of elderly people through computer vision. Artificial Life and Robotics, 28 (2023), 496–501. Springer. https://doi.org/10.1007/s10015-023-00882-y

Synopsis: Introduces a real-time HAR system using CNNs and pose estimation (OpenPose) for detecting falls, sitting, eating, sleeping, and exercising. Focuses on privacy by storing only activity logs.

Reliability: Published in Springer's Artificial Life and Robotics journal, peer-reviewed, with real-world experimental validation.

[4]

F. Xavier Gaya-Morey, Cristina Manresa-Yee, and José M. Buades-Rubio. 2024. Deep learning for computer vision based activity recognition and fall detection of the elderly: a systematic review. Applied Intelligence, 54 (2024), 8982–9007. Springer. https://doi.org/10.1007/s10489-024-05645-1

Synopsis: Systematic review of 151 studies (2019–2023) on deep learning methods for elderly HAR and fall detection. Discusses datasets, DL architectures, privacy issues, and deployment challenges.

Reliability: Comprehensive review article, peer-reviewed, highly reliable for state-of-the-art context.

[5]

Rahmat Ullah, Ikram Asghar, Saeed Akbar, Gareth Evans, Justus Vermaak, Abdulaziz Alblwi, and Amna Bamaqa. 2025. Vision-Based Activity Recognition for Unobtrusive Monitoring of the Elderly in Care Settings. Technologies, 13, 5 (2025), 184. MDPI. https://doi.org/10.3390/technologies13050184

Synopsis: Presents a lightweight vision-based monitoring system using frame differencing and anomaly detection. Focuses on unobtrusive, privacy-preserving monitoring in care homes.

Reliability: Published in MDPI's peer-reviewed Technologies journal. Open access, recent, and relevant for real-world deployment.
