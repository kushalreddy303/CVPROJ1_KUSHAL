Techniques for Activity Recognition

From classical machine learning to deep neural networks and explainable AI.

From Video to Understanding

Once sensors (RGB, depth, or infrared) and pose estimation frameworks provide input, the next step is to analyze the data and recognize human activities. This requires algorithms capable of interpreting movements, detecting patterns, and classifying them into meaningful actions like walking, sitting, exercising, or falling.

Researchers have explored many approaches, ranging from classic machine learning to deep learning and explainable AI.

Classical Machine Learning Approaches

Early systems relied on handcrafted features such as joint angles, body silhouettes, or optical flow. These features were then fed into algorithms like:

Support Vector Machines (SVMs)

Effective for binary classification (e.g., fall vs. no fall).

Decision Trees & Random Forests

Simple but effective for multi-class activity recognition.

Ensemble Methods

Combining multiple classifiers to boost accuracy [1].

Note: These methods are lightweight and interpretable, but they often struggle with complex real-world scenarios.

Deep Learning Approaches

Deep learning has revolutionized Human Activity Recognition (HAR) by learning features automatically from raw video or skeleton data.

1

Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs)

  • Great for extracting spatial features from images or video frames
  • Used to identify body posture, shapes, and spatial patterns [2]
2

Recurrent Neural Networks (RNNs) & LSTMs

  • Capture temporal patterns (sequences over time)
  • Example: distinguishing standing up from already standing
  • LSTMs are especially effective in handling long-term dependencies [4]
3

Graph Convolutional Networks (GCNs)

  • Treat skeleton keypoints as nodes in a graph
  • Learn spatial and temporal relationships between joints (e.g., hip → knee → ankle movement)
  • Highly efficient for skeleton-based activity recognition [4]

Anomaly Detection for Unusual Events

Sometimes, instead of classifying every possible action, the system learns what is "normal" behavior and flags deviations.

Normal Behavior

System learns typical daily patterns and activities.

Detect Anomalies

Flags deviations like prolonged inactivity or sudden falls [3].

Lightweight Models

Often implemented with unsupervised learning for rare events.

Advantage: Useful when collecting training data for rare events is difficult.

Explainable AI (XAI)

Trust is crucial in elderly care. Caregivers and families need to understand why an AI system raised an alert.

LIME

Local Interpretable Model-Agnostic Explanations

  • Highlights which video regions influenced the prediction [5]
  • Helps doctors, nurses, and family members trust the system
  • Identifies possible causes of misclassification (e.g., shadow mistaken for a fall)
AI GENERATED
Why It Matters: Explainability builds trust and helps identify system errors before they cause harm.

Key Takeaway

Activity recognition techniques range from classic ML (fast, simple, but limited) to deep learning and GCNs (powerful, data-hungry, state-of-the-art). Increasingly, researchers are combining methods, using pose estimation + deep learning + explainable AI to create systems that are accurate, efficient, and trustworthy.
