Introduction: Why Computer Vision for Elderly Care?

Exploring how AI-powered vision systems are transforming elderly monitoring, safety, and independence.

The Problem

The world's population is aging rapidly. According to the World Health Organization, by 2050 nearly 1 in 6 people will be over the age of 60. While people are living longer, many elderly individuals face challenges such as:

Falls and Injuries

Falls are the second leading cause of accidental injury deaths worldwide, with over 37 million serious falls each year.

Social Isolation

Many older adults live alone, without constant supervision or immediate access to help.

Limited Healthcare Staff

Hospitals and care facilities often struggle with staff shortages, making continuous monitoring difficult.

Key Insight: These issues highlight the urgent need for automated monitoring systems that can provide support without intruding on an elderly person's daily life.

Why Computer Vision?

Traditional monitoring methods often use wearable sensors or smart devices. While useful, they have major drawbacks:

Limitations of Wearables
  • Elderly people may forget or refuse to wear devices
  • Devices can be uncomfortable or expensive
  • Battery life and maintenance are ongoing issues
Advantages of Computer Vision
  • Cameras can observe activities unobtrusively
  • Vision-based systems detect postures, movements, and abnormal events like falls
  • AI algorithms can learn patterns, flag anomalies, and even explain why an alert was triggered

Unlike wearables, CV systems work in the background, making them well-suited for Ambient Assisted Living (AAL) environments [1] [2].
