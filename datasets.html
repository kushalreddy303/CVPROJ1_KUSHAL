Datasets for Elderly Activity Recognition & Fall Detection

Essential datasets for training and evaluating computer vision models in elderly care.

Why Datasets Matter

Robust activity recognition depends on representative, diverse datasets. For elderly monitoring, that means capturing daily living activities (ADLs) and rare but critical events like falls. Larger, well-annotated datasets improve generalization, reduce bias, and enable fair comparisons across methods.

Data Modalities

RGB Video

Rich detail, easy to deploy, but raises privacy concerns.

Depth

Encodes distance; enables skeleton extraction and better privacy than RGB.

Infrared (IR)/Thermal

Works in low light; silhouettes instead of identifiable faces.

Skeleton (2D/3D keypoints)

Compact, privacy-preserving representation of pose and motion.

Multimodal

Combinations (e.g., RGB + skeleton + IMU) improve robustness and reduce false alarms.

See Sensors & Vision page for modality trade-offs.

Key Datasets (What to Know & When to Use)

1

ETRI-Activity3D

Elderly-focused, home/robot view

Modalities: RGB video (+ depth & skeleton available)

Scope: ~112,620 samples, 55 actions (ADLs oriented), recorded at ~20 FPS in home-like settings from robot-height cameras

Use For: Elderly ADLs, realistic layouts, training deep models (CNN/GCN/LSTM)

Notes: Excellent scale and realism for elder care; supports pose and privacy-aware pipelines.

2

UP-FALL

Falls + ADLs, balanced classes

Modalities: RGB video, IMU sensors (accelerometer/gyroscope), sometimes audio

Scope: 5 fall types + 6 common activities

Use For: Joint vision + wearable fusion, benchmarking fall vs. non-fall and everyday actions

Notes: Great for multimodal fusion and evaluating false alarms.

3

UR Fall Detection Dataset (URFD)

Classic fall dataset

Modalities: RGB video

Scope: Staged falls and non-falls in controlled scenes

Use For: Baseline benchmarking of traditional and deep methods

Notes: Smaller/acted; useful for quick prototyping and pipeline sanity checks.

4

Le2i Fall Detection Dataset

Multiple indoor scenes

Modalities: RGB video from multiple indoor scenes (coffee room, home, office)

Scope: Staged falls and daily activities in varied environments

Use For: Testing generalization across rooms and camera angles

Notes: Diversity of backgrounds is helpful for robustness studies.

5

NTU RGB+D / NTU RGB+D 120

Large-scale HAR, not elderly-specific

Modalities: RGB, Depth, 3D skeleton

Use For: Skeleton-based methods (GCN/LSTM), pretraining on large pose data

Evaluation Protocols (How to Test Fairly)

Train/Test Split

Keep subjects disjoint to avoid identity leakage.

Cross-Subject

Train on one group, test on unseen people (more realistic).

Cross-View / Cross-Scene

Train on one view/room, test on another to assess robustness.

Metrics

Accuracy for balanced classes; F1-score, Precision/Recall, and ROC-AUC for imbalanced fall detection; Confusion Matrix to inspect false alarms vs. misses; Latency/FPS for real-time systems.

Ethics & Privacy

  • Prefer depth/IR or skeleton-only pipelines when possible.
  • Anonymize faces/backgrounds or run inference on-device to avoid sending raw video to the cloud.
  • Obtain informed consent; follow local data protection rules; minimize retention of raw identifiable footage.
